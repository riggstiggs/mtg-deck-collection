# Terra, Herald of Hope Deck Guide

## Commander Strategy
**Terra, Herald of Hope**
*   **Role:** The Combat Reanimator & Self-Miller.
*   **Goal:** To attack aggressively with Terra to trigger her "Trance" ability, filling your graveyard and then returning fallen allies to the battlefield.
*   **The Engine:**
    1.  **Trance:** At the beginning of combat, Terra mills two cards and gains Flying. This provides both evasion and the necessary graveyard fuel for her second ability.
    2.  **Combat Reanimation:** Whenever Terra deals combat damage to a player, you can pay {2} to return a creature with power 3 or less from your graveyard to the battlefield.
    3.  **Mardu Synergy:** The deck utilizes the colors of war (Red, White, Black) to control the board and recycle resources from the graveyard.

## 1. The Small But Mighty (Reanimate Targets)
*Low-power creatures that provide high utility when returned by Terra.*
*   **Celes, Rune Knight:** Likely provides spell-based protection or disruption.
*   **Locke, Treasure Hunter:** Provides card advantage by finding artifacts or drawing cards on attack.
*   **Mog, Moogle Warrior:** Likely a utility creature that buffs the team or provides minor mana advantage.
*   **Shadow, Mysterious Assassin:** An evasive threat that can remove key creatures.
*   **Priest of Fell Rites:** A reanimation spell on a body that Terra can bring back repeatedly.

## 2. High-Impact Threats & Antagonists
*Powerful cards to finish the game or control the board.*
*   **Kefka, Dancing Mad:** The primary antagonist. Likely provides chaotic board states or punishes opponents for casting spells or attacking.
*   **The Warring Triad:** A massive threat representing the primal magic of FF VI, likely dominating the battlefield.
*   **Sepulchral Primordial:** Steals the best creatures from your opponents' graveyards.
*   **Archfiend of Depravity:** Shuts down "go-wide" strategies by forcing opponents to sacrifice creatures down to two.

## 3. Final Fantasy Flavor & Tools
*Unique cards from the Final Fantasy VI theme.*
*   **Phoenix Down:** A classic FF item. Likely an artifact that can be sacrificed to return a creature to the battlefield.
*   **The Falcon, Airship Restored:** Provides evasion and potentially acts as a vehicle or mana source.
*   **Coin of Fate / Setzer, Wandering Gambler:** Representing the gambling theme of Setzer, providing high-risk/high-reward effects.
*   **Summon: Esper Valigarmanda:** A powerful "summon" spell that deals damage or provides a mass-freeze effect.

## 4. Reanimation & Recursion
*Alternative ways to bring back your creatures.*
*   **Reanimate / Stitch Together:** Classic black spells to bring back your biggest threats (like *Combustible Gearhulk*).
*   **Sun Titan:** Recovers any small permanent (lands, artifacts, or creatures) on attack or ETB.
*   **Rise of the Dark Realms:** The ultimate finisher, reanimating every creature from every graveyard.

## 5. Interaction & Removal
*   **Ruinous Ultimatum:** One of the best board wipes in Mardu, destroying all nonland permanents your opponents control.
*   **Crackling Doom:** Forces each opponent to sacrifice their largest creature while dealing damage.
*   **Bedevil / Mortify:** Versatile instant-speed removal for creatures, artifacts, and enchantments.

## Winning the Game
*   **Terra Voltron:** Buff Terra with equipment and repeatedly reanimate utility creatures while chipping away at life totals.
*   **Antagonist Lock:** Use *Kefka* and *Archfiend of Depravity* to starve opponents of resources while you rebuild from the yard.
*   **Mass Reanimation:** End the game with a huge *Rise of the Dark Realms* or *Ruinous Ultimatum*.
