# Goldfish Log: Etali, Primal Conqueror — "Primal Budget Blitz"

**Goal:** Track how quickly the deck can generate 7 mana ({5}{R}{R}) with 2 red sources and cast Etali, Primal Conqueror.
**Sessions:** 5 games
**Date:** 2026-03-16

---

## Key Casting Requirements
- **Etali costs:** {5}{R}{R} = 7 total mana, **minimum 2 red sources required**
- Baseline (no ramp): Turn 7
- Target (good ramp): Turn 4–5
- Red mana note: Only 12 Mountains in 99 cards (~12%) — this is the primary color-fixing risk

## Key Findings

### ✅ What's Working
1. **Mana acceleration is functional.** When the deck draws ramp (Llanowar Elves, Utopia Sprawl, Wild Growth, Rampant Growth, Dire-Strain Rampage), it consistently reaches 7 mana by turns 4–5. Game 3 hit turn 4 — a full 3 turns ahead of the baseline.
2. **Dire-Strain Rampage is secretly the MVP ramp spell.** It solves both the land count and the red-mana problem simultaneously by fetching Forest + Mountain (two different basic types). It should be prioritized in every opening hand decision.
3. **Red mana is manageable when Rampage is available.** Multiple games had thin red (only 1 Mountain in view), and Rampage bailed them out every time.

### ⚠️ Critical Problems Identified

1. **Etali never appeared in the top 20 cards (0/5 games).** This is statistically expected — 1 copy in 99 cards means roughly an 18% chance of appearing in the first 20. The deck has NO way to tutor for Etali, meaning games average 15–25 turns of development before the commander arrives naturally. In a real 4-player game, you'd likely get Etali off the command zone 2–3 times but at increasing commander tax.

2. **Red mana is dangerously thin (12 Mountains = 12% of deck).** Three of the five games had only 1 Mountain visible in the top 20 cards. Without Dire-Strain Rampage or early Mountain draws, the deck physically cannot cast Etali's {R}{R} requirement. Upgrading to 14–16 effective red sources (Command Tower, Stomping Ground, Cinder Glade + more Mountains) would significantly reduce variance.

3. **Sacrifice spells (Fling, Collateral Damage, Rupture, Metamorphosis) are dead draws in the early game.** Game 5 showed 3 of these in an opening hand — they collectively did nothing for 6 turns. These cards need a creature to function and during the ramp phase there are zero creatures. They shine late (when Etali is threatened) but hurt early hands.

4. **Temple of the False God is a liability in early hands.** It appeared in Games 1 and 2 as an opening land that did absolutely nothing until Turn 4–5. Any hand where Temple is the 3rd or 4th land is effectively running fewer functional mana sources.

5. **No protection for Etali once it arrives.** The commander costs 7 mana and triggers on ETB. After ramping for 5–7 turns to finally cast it, a single removal spell wastes all that setup with no way to respond. Lightning Greaves / Swiftfoot Boots would be transformative.
