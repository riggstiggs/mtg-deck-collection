# Deck Guide: Etali, Primal Conqueror — "Primal Budget Blitz"

## Commander Strategy
**Etali, Primal Conqueror**
*   **Archetype:** Gruul (Red/Green) Stompy / Spell Theft
*   **Bracket:** 2
*   **Core Goal:** Ramp as hard and fast as possible, land Etali ({5}{R}{R} = 7 mana) ahead of curve, and trigger its powerful ETB ability — when Etali enters the battlefield, each player exiles cards until they hit a nonland card, and you may cast any of those spells for free. Copy effects (Cursed Mirror, Flamerush Rider, Jaxis) re-trigger this ETB for massive additional value. Finish with sacrifice-into-damage spells (Fling, Rupture) when Etali is threatened. Etali can also transform into **Etali, Primal Sickness** (indestructible, deals poison counters on combat damage) for an alternate win condition via paying {9}{G/P}.

> **Budget Note:** This deck was built as an extreme budget challenge (≈$25 or less). Card choices reflect cost constraints rather than optimal power. Expect significant room for upgrades — see the roadmap below.

> ✅ **Color Identity Confirmed:** Etali, Primal Conqueror is Gruul (R/G) — mana cost {5}{R}{R} with a {G/P} transform ability. All cards in this list are legal.

> ✅ **Clifftop Lookout Confirmed:** This is a real card — a {2}{G} Frog Scout creature (not a land). Its ETB reveals cards from the top of your library until you find a land and puts it into play tapped. It has been moved to the Ramp section.

## 🛠️ Card Explanations

### 🌄 Lands (52 Cards)
> *52 lands is very high but intentional for the budget constraint — basics are free/nearly free and ensure consistent Etali casts. Note: Clifftop Lookout was originally listed here but is a creature; it has been moved to Ramp.*

*   **35 Forest / 12 Mountain:** Massive basic land count keeps the budget down and supports the numerous green/red mana sources.
*   **Cinder Glade:** Budget G/R dual that ETBs untapped with two or more basics — almost always live in this deck.
*   **Dining Room:** Duskmourn room — verify exact ETB/activation effects. Likely provides some utility or mana production.
*   **Moldering Gym/Weight Room:** Duskmourn double-sided room — adds a dual utility land slot at low budget cost.
*   **Rogue's Passage:** Key evasion land — makes Etali unblockable to guarantee combat damage pressure and support Etali, Primal Storm's attack trigger.
*   **Temple of the False God:** Effectively taps for 2 mana once you have 5+ lands, which this deck reaches quickly.

### 🌿 Mana Ramp (22 Cards)
*   **Clifftop Lookout:** {2}{G} Frog Scout — ETB reveals cards from the top of your library until you find a land and puts it into play tapped. Essentially a budget Wood Elves variant that hits any basic. Originally miscategorized as a land in the source list.
*   **Arbor Elf:** Untaps a Forest (or Forest-typed dual) for effectively 2 mana on turn 2. Pairs beautifully with Utopia Sprawl/Wild Growth.
*   **Llanowar Elves:** Classic 1-drop mana dork — puts Etali a full turn ahead of schedule.
*   **Utopia Sprawl / Wild Growth:** Land auras that turn a Forest into a 3-mana land. Combo with Arbor Elf for 4 mana on turn 2.
*   **Explore:** Draw a card and take an extra land drop — cheap tempo ramp.
*   **Rampant Growth:** Reliable 2-mana basic land search, mana-fixing double duty in Gruul.
*   **Search for Tomorrow:** Suspend 1 on turn 1 to get a free basic on turn 3 — the best budget ramp spell.
*   **Sakura-Tribe Elder:** Blocks, then sacrifices to fetch a basic. Classic and efficient.
*   **Elvish Rejuvenator:** Reveal top 5, put a land into play — hits basics reliably in this land-heavy deck.
*   **Springbloom Druid:** ETB fetch two basics (both enter tapped) — great with Rampaging Baloths.
*   **Wood Elves:** Fetches any Forest (including Cinder Glade) and ETBs untapped — very efficient at 3 mana.
*   **Yavimaya Dryad:** Fetches a Forest for any player — useful politically or for personal acceleration.
*   **Yavimaya Elder:** ETB fetches two basics (to hand), replaces itself on death — incredible value engine.
*   **Solemn Simulacrum:** Fetches a basic, draws on death. The quintessential Commander ramp/draw creature.
*   **Greater Tanuki:** Reach body in a pinch; Channel ability fetches a basic for 3G — flexible.
*   **Krosan Tusker:** Cycle for GG to tutor any basic land to hand — effectively a 2-mana ramp spell with a body backup.
*   **Nissa's Renewal:** 6-mana sorcery that puts three basics into play and gains 7 life — expensive but explosive in the late game.
*   **Vastwood Surge:** Kick it for 4 basics and pump all creatures — good late-game mana sink.
*   **Dire-Strain Rampage:** Destroys a target artifact/enchantment and fetches two basics — budget Cultivate with removal upside.
*   **Goblin Anarchomancer:** Reduces the cost of every red or green spell by 1 — cuts Etali's cost from {5}{R}{R} (7 mana) to effectively 6, meaningful when you're racing to cast a 7-drop.
*   **Metamorphosis:** Sacrifice a creature to add mana equal to its converted mana cost + 1 — budget emergency mana to hard-cast Etali if needed.

### 🃏 Card Draw & Filtering (8 Cards)
*   **Harmonize:** Budget sorcery-speed draw 3. Simple and effective.
*   **Return of the Wildspeaker:** Draw cards equal to the greatest power among your non-Human creatures — Etali is a massive dinosaur, so this draws a large number. Can also give +3/+3 and trample as an instant combat trick.
*   **Escape to the Wilds:** Exile top 5, cast any number from exile plus take an extra land drop — massive hand reload.
*   **Tireless Tracker:** Generates Clue tokens on landfall — the high land count means this draws many cards over time.
*   **Magnifying Glass:** Scry 1 each upkeep, then tap to sacrifice for a Clue draw — slow but budget filtering.
*   **Sunset Pyramid:** Charge counter-based scry/draw — limited but functional filtering on a budget.
*   **Thrill of Possibility:** Instant-speed loot — discard a land, draw 2. Best in hand-size crunch situations.
*   **Evolutionary Leap:** Sacrifice a creature to dig for another creature — protects Etali value by converting it into something else when threatened.

### ⚡ Copy Effects & Haste Enablers (3 Cards)
> *Since Etali's trigger fires on ETB (not on attack), these cards re-trigger it by creating copies. Each copy entering the battlefield fires its own ETB — potentially stealing 3-4 spells per turn cycle.*

*   **Cursed Mirror:** ETBs as a hasty copy of Etali — the copy's ETB immediately fires, stealing another opponent's spell. On your next upkeep it reverts to a Mirror, but the ETB value is already banked.
*   **Flamerush Rider:** Creates a hasty token copy of Etali when it attacks — that token's ETB fires, triggering additional spell theft. Token is exiled at end of combat but the spells are already cast.
*   **Jaxis, the Troublemaker:** Blitz out a copy of Etali for {R} — the copy's ETB fires for another round of free spells, then draws a card when it dies. Incredible budget efficiency for re-triggering Etali.

### 💥 Sacrifice & Damage Spells (3 Cards)
*   **Fling:** Instant-speed sacrifice Etali when it's about to die or when you need a finishing blow — converts Etali's massive power into lethal damage targeting a player.
*   **Collateral Damage:** 1-mana version of Fling — cheapest way to convert Etali into damage before it's removed.
*   **Rupture:** Sacrifice a creature to deal its power to each other creature and each player — turns Etali or a large token into a table-wide sweeper and player damage spike.

### 🗡️ Removal & Interaction (3 Cards)
*   **Reclamation Sage:** ETB destroys an artifact or enchantment — staple green interaction on a body.
*   **Dissection Tools:** Duskmourn artifact — provides removal or damage-based utility (verify exact text on Scryfall).
*   **Ezuri's Predation:** Creates 4/4 tokens that fight each opponent's creature — expensive but can wipe a board of mid-size threats.

### 🏆 Value Engines & Win Conditions (8 Cards)
*   **Etali, Primal Storm:** The original Etali ({4}{R}{R}{G}) — its trigger fires on attack (not ETB), layering additional spell theft on top of Etali Conqueror's ETB. Running both creates two different trigger windows and gives you a fallback win condition.
*   **Chaos Wand:** Tap to exile the top card of an opponent's library and cast it if it's an instant or sorcery — slow but free value every turn.
*   **Dragonlair Spider:** Reach body that creates a 1/1 Insect token whenever an opponent casts a spell — discourages opponents from developing their boards and generates blockers/chump attackers.
*   **Rampaging Baloths:** Landfall — creates a 4/4 Beast token every time a land enters. The high land count and fetch spells mean this generates an army quickly.
*   **Skullwinder:** ETB returns a card from your graveyard — and an opponent's graveyard. Political tool and budget Eternal Witness.
*   **Timeless Witness:** Retrace version of Eternal Witness — returns any card from your graveyard and can be recurred with retrace to replay lands.
*   **Ulvenwald Hydra:** Massive reach creature whose size matches your land count (often 10+); ETB searches for any land — tutors Rogue's Passage to guarantee Etali evasion.
*   **Hit the Mother Lode:** Treasure generation — creates tokens to bankroll a late-game Etali recast or power through a mana-thin turn.

## 🚀 Future Roadmap: Upgrades & Considerations

### 💡 High-Impact Tech to Consider
*These cards are not currently in the deck but are strong upgrade candidates given the deck's strategy.*

*   **Lightning Greaves / Swiftfoot Boots:** Free/cheap haste AND protection for Etali — the single highest-impact upgrade category missing from this deck.
*   **Sol Ring / Arcane Signet:** The two most important mana rocks in Commander — missing here purely for budget. Both under $2 and should be first additions.
*   **Strionic Resonator:** Copies Etali's ETB trigger for {2} — doubles the spell theft on the same cast without needing a second copy of Etali.
*   **Selvala, Heart of the Wilds:** Taps for mana based on the greatest power — Etali's 7 power generates 7 mana. Explosive ramp that replaces multiple budget pieces.
*   **Cultivate / Kodama's Reach:** Standard 3-mana dual-fetch spells — strictly better than some of the slower budget ramp here.
*   **Temur Sabertooth:** Returns Etali to hand to dodge removal and replay it — protects the commander while enabling replays.
*   **Command Tower / Stomping Ground:** Basic dual land upgrades — Command Tower in particular should replace a basic immediately.
*   **Chaos Warp:** Red instant-speed removal for anything — cheap and excellent interaction this deck sorely lacks.
*   **Aggravated Assault:** Extra combat steps let you re-attack with Etali, Primal Storm (attack trigger) and deal additional combat damage to charge toward the Etali, Primal Sickness poison win. Less impactful on Etali Conqueror's ETB since that fires once on entry, not each attack.
