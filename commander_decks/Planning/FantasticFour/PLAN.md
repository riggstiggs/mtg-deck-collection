## The core mechanic (why "4s" is the whole deck)

The Fantastic Four triggers on ETB **and whenever you cast a spell with power 4,
toughness 4, OR mana value 4** — pick one mode not yet chosen this turn:
- Make a 0/4 Wall (defender)
- Deal 3 to each opponent
- Two +1/+1 counters on a creature
- Draw a card

So the deckbuilding constraint IS the theme: **maximize spells that are power 4,
toughness 4, or MV 4.** Every trigger = a mode = value. That's the engine.

## Design principles (from JAMIE.md)

- **4s first.** Prioritize cards that hit power 4 / toughness 4 / MV 4. This is the
  build-around and the flavor (the "4s" thing with Mark). Sentiment = mechanic.
- **Green does the heavy lifting** for ramp + 4-color fixing + card draw.
- **HOST constraint:** commander triggers are the fun decisions; keep the REST of
  the deck low-fuss so Jamie isn't drowning in choices while she's up hosting.
- **Value/go-wide, not combo.** Walls + counters + draw. No stax, no hard control,
  no infinite combo. "Quiet until it isn't."
- **Recastable commander** — protect it / keep recasts affordable (it's only 4 MV,
  which is itself a 4, nice).

## ⭐ Make it feel DIFFERENT (Jamie's steer, 2026-07-19)

Four-color (RGWU = anything but black) is the point — use the FULL range, don't
default to the same green-Simic cards Jamie already runs in 3 other decks. Green
YES, but FRESH green — new toys, not Toski/Forgotten Ancient/Second Harvest again.

**Build around the FOUR MEMBERS as sub-themes** (flavor = organizing principle,
and each maps to a color + a commander mode):

| Member | Theme | Mechanical direction | Color lean |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mister Fantastic** | STRETCH | flexibility, modal/adaptable spells, reach, elastic answers | U (splash) |
| **Invisible Woman** | INVISIBLE | protection, hexproof/shroud, phasing, force fields, blink/flicker | U/W |
| **Human Torch** | FIRE | damage/reach, haste, aggression (feeds the "3 to each opp" mode) | R |
| **The Thing** | BEASTLY | big tough bodies, +1/+1 counters, trample, toughness-4 walls that hit | G/R |

Card hunts go PILLAR BY PILLAR, not generic green value. Avoid repeating Jamie's
existing staples where a fresh equivalent exists.

⭐⭐ **BUILD THE BEST WORKING DECK — do NOT build around owned cards.** Jamie's
collection/store = nice-to-have for saving money, NOT a constraint. Best cards that
fit the plan win, owned or not; tag owned/buy afterward for info only. Right first,
budget later-if-needed. (Respect real prefs: no painlands, no $200 fetchlands,
host-friendly, flavor-only-if-it-helps, hard NOs.)

⭐ **FLAVOR MUST EARN ITS SLOT (Jamie, 2026-07-19):** a flavorful card only goes in
if it ALSO does the job — for this deck that means hitting a 4 (power/toughness/MV 4)
to trigger the commander, or otherwise advancing the plan. Do NOT add a flavor card
that's off-plan (e.g. The Thing, Ben Grimm is {5}{G} 7/7 = MV6/P7/T7, hits NO 4 →
CUT). "4s over flavor," and flavor only when it's also a 4 / on-plan.

### Build decisions (Jamie, 2026-07-19)

- **"Assemble the team" — LEAN IN.** The 4 member cards are cornerstones, each
  leading its theme. Using precon cards is FINE — Jamie may buy 2nd copies or
  detonate the precon to build this; don't optimize around card availability.
- **The 4 members map to themes & are RGWU-costed:**
  - Mister Fantastic, Reed Richards {3}{U} MV4, Reach, draw when tokens enter — STRETCH
  - Invisible Woman {2}{W}, makes 0/4 Walls, RGWU→unblockable — INVISIBLE

  - The Thing {5}{G}, trample, +4 counters, RGWU→double counters — BEASTLY

    turn** → run enough noncreature spells + strong fixing for these to fire.
- **MANA / PIPS:** double-pip a color is OK; **avoid TRIPLE-pip** (too hard in 4c).
- **LANDS:** lean HARD into dual lands. Jamie owns **all Strixhaven duals**
  (Campuses + the SNC/MID-era duals?) and will upload her store xlsx to pull
  lands she already owns FIRST before buying. Build the manabase dual-heavy.

  Door of Destinies, Goreclaw (Goreclaw = Temur/{2}{G}{G} MV4 — big-creature payoff, BEASTLY fit).
