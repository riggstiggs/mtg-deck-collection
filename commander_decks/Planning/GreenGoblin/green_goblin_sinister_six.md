# Deck Guide: Norman Osborn / Green Goblin — "The Sinister Six"

## Commander Strategy
**Norman Osborn / Green Goblin**
*   **Archetype:** Grixis (Blue/Black/Red) Wheels / Discard Madness / Graveyard Value
*   **Bracket:** 3 (2 Game Changers: Cyclonic Rift, Fierce Guardianship)
*   **Core Goal:** Use Norman Osborn's scheming abilities to fuel a relentless discard engine — wheeling hands away and filling the graveyard with value — then transform into Green Goblin to punish opponents with the chaos generated. Every wheel spell triggers a chain of discard payoffs (Bone Miser, Glint-Horn Buccaneer, Burning Vengeance), while the graveyard fuels reanimation and cast-from-graveyard engines (Chainer, Acererak, Secrets of the Dead). A premium interaction suite backed by Mana Drain, Fierce Guardianship, Deflecting Swat, and Cyclonic Rift protects the strategy from disruption and punishes opponents who tap out.

### 🧨 How to Pilot the Sinister Six
1.  **Early Game (Turns 1–3):** Deploy mana acceleration (Sol Ring, Talismans, Dark Ritual) and land one discard payoff — Bone Miser, Glint-Horn Buccaneer, or Burning Vengeance — before casting Norman Osborn. Leave mana up for Mana Drain or Fierce Guardianship whenever possible.
2.  **Mid Game (Turns 4–6):** Begin wheeling. Each Windfall or Tolarian Winds with Bone Miser online generates treasure + card advantage. With Glint-Horn Buccaneer, each discarded card pings all opponents. Stack multiple payoffs before a wheel for exponential returns.
3.  **Late Game (Turns 7+):** Rebuild from the graveyard. Chainer Nightmare Adept lets you cast any creature from the graveyard with haste each turn. Acererak's dungeon-delving generates ongoing value. Recover with Living Death-style reanimation lines from the graveyard loaded by wheels.

## 🛠️ Card Explanations

### 🕷️ The Spider-Man Villain Package (6 Cards)
*Thematic Spider-Man Universes Beyond cards that support the discard and chaos strategy.*

*   **Green Goblin, Nemesis:** The alternate version of the commander — a standalone threat that embodies the deck's chaotic disruption identity. Provides another avenue of pressure independent of Norman Osborn's transformation.
*   **Behold the Sinister Six!:** The deck's flagship thematic card. Assembles the Sinister Six villain alliance — likely tutors or assembles a suite of payoff creatures to overwhelm opponents in a single turn. The six-villain assembly synergizes with the creatures and discard payoffs already in the deck.
*   **Carnage, Crimson Chaos:** Carnage from Marvel's symbiote villains — a chaos-enabling threat that generates cascading damage or disruption effects. Fits the deck's "maximum chaos" gameplan.
*   **Doctor Octopus, Master Planner:** A scheming creature that likely tutors or manipulates the top of the library — Master Planner suggests card selection or a plan-ahead ability that digs for wheel spells or payoffs.
*   **Electro, Assaulting Battery:** Electro's electrical chaos deals direct damage — likely pings opponents or amplifies the damage-per-discard axis already built by Glint-Horn Buccaneer. A redundant source of incremental pressure.
*   **Pumpkin Bombs:** Green Goblin's signature weapon — likely an instant or sorcery that deals damage or creates a discard-adjacent effect, fitting the deck's identity of chaotic one-shot bursts of value.

### 🌀 Wheel & Discard Engine (9 Cards)
*The core of the strategy — spells that cycle the entire hand, filling the graveyard and triggering every discard payoff simultaneously.*

*   **Windfall:** {2}{U} sorcery. Each player discards their hand then draws cards equal to the greatest number discarded. The premier wheel in Commander — with three opponents, Windfall often draws 5–7 cards while triggering every discard payoff simultaneously. With Bone Miser, generates Treasure for each non-land discarded.
*   **Faithless Looting:** {R} sorcery — draw 2, discard 2. Flashback {2}{R}. The cheapest targeted discard in the deck. Flashback makes it a two-for-one over the game — two discard triggers at different points.
*   **Frantic Search:** {2}{U} instant — draw 2, discard 2, untap up to three lands. The untap effect means it effectively costs zero or negative mana mid-game. Instant-speed wheel that generates discard triggers on opponents' end steps.
*   **Tolarian Winds:** {U} sorcery — discard your hand, draw that many cards. A one-mana wheel that discards every card for a fresh hand. With Bone Miser, converts a large hand into an equal number of Treasures before refilling.
*   **Demand Answers:** {1}{R} instant — discard a card, draw two. Cheap and efficient targeted discard. Trading one card for two while triggering discard payoffs is pure card advantage.
*   **Unexpected Windfall:** {2}{R}{R} instant — discard a card, draw two, create two Treasure tokens. Instant-speed discard plus Treasure plus card advantage — the burst mana from Treasures often funds the next wheel spell on the same turn.
*   **Big Score:** {4}{R} sorcery — discard a card, draw two, create two Treasure tokens. The pricier sorcery-speed sibling to Unexpected Windfall. The higher mana cost is offset by the two Treasure tokens providing immediate payback.
*   **Will of the Jeskai:** A discard-and-draw effect in Grixis colors — refuels the hand while triggering payoffs and filling the graveyard with fuel for Chainer and reanimation lines.
*   **Into the Flood Maw:** {3}{U} spell — a targeted discard or hand disruption effect that also fills the graveyard. Disrupts a key opponent's hand while generating the deck's primary resource.

### ⚡ Discard Payoffs (7 Cards)
*Permanents that convert every discard trigger into damage, cards, mana, or board presence — the engine that makes wheels lethal.*

*   **Bone Miser:** {4}{B} creature. Whenever you discard a land, create a Swamp token. Whenever you discard a nonland, create a Treasure. Whenever you discard a creature, create a 2/2 Zombie. A full wheel with Bone Miser converts every card discarded into a resource — Treasures from spells fuel future wheels, Zombies from creatures build a board, and Swamps from lands accelerate the mana base.
*   **Glint-Horn Buccaneer:** {2}{R}{R} creature. Whenever you discard a card, Glint-Horn deals 1 damage to each opponent. Has haste and can be tapped to draw a card then discard a card. Each card discarded in a wheel pings all three opponents simultaneously — a Windfall discarding five cards deals 5 damage to each opponent from this card alone.
*   **Brallin, Skyshark Rider:** {3}{R} creature. Whenever you discard a card, put a +1/+1 counter on Brallin and it deals 1 damage to each opponent. Grows while dealing damage — a secondary Glint-Horn Buccaneer effect that scales up over the game as wheels accumulate.
*   **Burning Vengeance:** {2}{R} enchantment. Whenever you cast an instant or sorcery from your graveyard, Burning Vengeance deals 2 damage to any target. With Faithless Looting's flashback and Chainer enabling graveyard casts, this pings opponents for 2 every time you reach back into the graveyard.
*   **Ghostly Pilferer:** {1}{U} creature. Whenever an opponent draws a card (except their first each turn), you may pay {1}{U} to draw a card. Whenever it's discarded, draw a card. Perfect in a wheel deck where opponents are always drawing cards — generates passive card draw while the discard safety net draws you a card if it's wheeled away.
*   **Surly Badgersaur:** {2}{R} creature. Whenever you discard a land, create a 2/2 Bear token. Whenever you discard a nonland, Surly Badgersaur deals 2 damage to any target. A damage-dealing Bone Miser variant — nonland discards deal 2 damage to any target, distributing lethal damage across opponents over a game of wheels.
*   **Archfiend of Ifnir:** {3}{B}{B} creature. Flying, cycling {2}{B}. Whenever you discard a card, put a -1/-1 counter on each creature an opponent controls. Wheels wipe opponents' creature boards — discarding five cards in a single Windfall puts five -1/-1 counters on each of their creatures, devastating tokens and small creatures.

### 🪦 Graveyard Synergies & Reanimation (7 Cards)
*Cards that reward a graveyard full of discarded threats or return them to the battlefield.*

*   **Chainer, Nightmare Adept:** {2}{B}{R} creature. Creatures you cast from your graveyard have haste. Once per turn when you discard a card, you may cast a creature from your graveyard. A one-card engine that converts every discard trigger into a free creature cast from the graveyard with haste — with Burning Vengeance online, each graveyard creature cast also deals 2 damage.
*   **Acererak the Archlich:** {2}{B} creature. Venture into the dungeon on ETB, then return to hand unless all dungeons are completed. Acererak generates dungeon value repeatedly across the game — each ETB ventures, drawing cards and generating Zombie tokens from Dungeon of the Mad Mage before eventually completing the dungeon for a game-ending effect.
*   **Bringer of the Last Gift:** {5}{B}{B} creature. A powerful reanimation threat that serves as either a reanimation target or a reanimation engine — its high power enters the graveyard via discarding and gets recurred with the deck's reanimation package.
*   **River Kelpie:** {3}{U}{U} creature. Whenever a permanent is put onto the battlefield from a graveyard, draw a card. Whenever a player casts a spell from a graveyard, draw a card. A draw engine built specifically for graveyard-matters decks — every Animate Dead, Reanimate, or Chainer-enabled cast draws a card.
*   **Secrets of the Dead:** {2}{U} enchantment. Whenever you cast a spell from a graveyard, draw a card. A redundant River Kelpie effect on an enchantment — combined with Chainer and Flashback cards, draws a card every time the graveyard is used as a resource.
*   **Animate Dead:** {1}{B} enchantment aura. Return target creature card from any graveyard to the battlefield attached to Animate Dead. The most efficient reanimation in black — two mana to reanimate any creature from any graveyard, using opponents' discarded threats against them.
*   **Reanimate:** {B} sorcery. Put target creature from any graveyard onto the battlefield under your control; you lose life equal to its converted mana cost. One-mana reanimation of any creature at the cost of life. With wheels loading every graveyard, Reanimate steals the best creature discarded by any opponent for a single black mana.

### 🎭 Value Creatures & Draw Engines (10 Cards)
*Creatures that generate ongoing mana, cards, or discard synergies throughout the game.*

*   **Birgi, God of Storytelling / Harnfel, Horn of Bounty:** {2}{R} — Birgi: whenever you cast a spell, add {R}. Harnfel (back face): discard a card to exile the top two cards of your library and you may cast them this turn. Birgi reduces the effective cost of every wheel and payoff spell, enabling multi-spell turns. Harnfel converts discards into impulsive draws — two sources of card advantage on a single card.
*   **Neheb, Dreadhorde Champion:** {3}{R} creature. Whenever Neheb deals combat damage to a player, discard any number of cards and draw that many cards. You add {R} to your mana pool for each card discarded this way. An attacking wheel on a 5/4 body — connecting with Neheb draws a fresh hand and generates massive red mana to cast everything at once.
*   **Vohar, Vodalian Desecrator:** {U}{B} legendary creature. When it ETBs, mill two cards. {1}{U/B}: exile an instant or sorcery from your graveyard, then you may cast a copy of it. A repeatable graveyard spell engine — exiles and recopies your best wheel spells for repeated triggers of every discard payoff.
*   **Rona, Herald of Invasion / Rona, Tolarian Obliterator:** {1}{U} — Herald side: whenever you cast a legendary spell, draw a card then discard a card. Tap to draw then discard a card. The discard trigger fires on every legendary cast and as a free tap ability — a reliable per-turn discard payoff that also digs through the deck.
*   **Likeness Looter:** {1}{U} creature. {1}{U}: discard a card and draw a card. Can become a copy of a creature you discarded for {2}{U}{U}. A free looter on a stick — controllable discard triggers on demand to activate Glint-Horn, Bone Miser, or Brallin whenever you have open mana.
*   **Psychic Frog:** {U}{B} creature. Whenever you discard a card, put a +1/+1 counter on Psychic Frog and you may draw a card. Flying. A growing evasive threat that rewards every discard with a counter and a card draw — becomes enormous across a game of wheels.
*   **Bog Witch:** {2}{B} creature. {T}, discard a card: add {B}{B}{B} to your mana pool. Sacrifice to add {B}{B}{B}. Converts a discard into three black mana — activating Bog Witch before a wheel generates the mana to cast Reanimate or Animate Dead with the creatures discarded in that same wheel.
*   **Skirge Familiar:** {4}{B} creature. Whenever you discard a card, add {B} to your mana pool. A mana engine for the discard deck — discarding five cards in a wheel generates five black mana, often funding the next graveyard spell immediately after the wheel resolves.
*   **Anger:** {3}{R} creature. As long as Anger is in your graveyard and you control a Mountain, creatures you control have haste. The enabler for the whole creature package — put Anger in the graveyard via wheel or discard, and every creature you control (or reanimate via Chainer) gains haste immediately.
*   **High Fae Trickster:** {1}{U} creature. An evasive flash threat that disrupts opponents while digging for key wheel spells. Provides card selection and a relevant body in the early turns.

### 🛡️ Premium Interaction Suite (9 Cards)
*A high-quality interaction package that protects the discard engine and punishes opponent tapping out.*

*   **Fierce Guardianship:** ⚠️ **Game Changer.** {2}{U} instant — if you control your commander, counter target noncreature spell without paying its mana cost. A free counterspell the moment Norman Osborn is on the battlefield. Protects wheels from being countered, protects the commander from removal, and taxes opponents into leaving mana up.
*   **Cyclonic Rift:** ⚠️ **Game Changer.** {1}{U} instant — Overload {6}{U} to return all nonland permanents you don't control to their owners' hands. The single most powerful board reset in Commander. One-sided wipes an entire board state while leaving your discard payoffs intact — the setup for a lethal wheel turn.
*   **Mana Drain:** {U}{U} instant — counter target spell; at the beginning of your next main phase, add that spell's converted mana cost in {C} to your mana pool. Counter a key spell and immediately bank the mana into the next wheel or reanimation spell. The premium two-mana counter in the format.
*   **Deflecting Swat:** {2}{R} instant — if you control your commander, redirect target spell or ability without paying its mana cost. A free redirect the moment Norman Osborn is in play. Turns targeted removal on the commander into a lethal weapon pointed at an opponent's key permanent.
*   **Deadly Rollick:** {3}{B} instant — if you control your commander, exile target creature without paying its mana cost. A free, unconditional exile removal spell. Kills any creature at instant speed at zero cost while the commander is on board.
*   **Swan Song:** {U} instant — counter target enchantment, instant, or sorcery spell; that spell's controller creates a 2/2 Bird token. The cheapest counterspell in the deck. Counters problematic enchantments like Rhystic Study or Smothering Tithe, and stops key wheel hate. The 2/2 Bird is irrelevant in this power level.
*   **Strix Serenade:** {1}{U}{U} instant — a counterspell variant that counters and provides additional value, likely drawing a card or exiling the countered spell. A premium counter that generates card advantage beyond just stopping the spell.
*   **Toxic Deluge:** {2}{B} sorcery — pay X life; all creatures get -X/-X until end of turn. Scales with how much life you're willing to pay. Clears an entire board of creatures including indestructible ones (unlike most board wipes). Essential mass removal for any Grixis deck.
*   **Lethal Scheme:** {3}{B} instant — creatures you control connive X, where X is the number of creatures tapped to pay the cost. Convoke to reduce the cost, then connive (draw and discard) with each creature — every connive trigger activates all discard payoffs. Removal plus discard engine in one card.

### 🗡️ Removal & Utility Spells (4 Cards)
*   **Bitter Triumph:** {1}{B} instant — destroy target creature or planeswalker; you lose 3 life unless you discard a card. The discard mode is usually correct in this deck — losing 3 life is irrelevant when you gain 3 discard triggers from Glint-Horn or Bone Miser in return.
*   **Snap:** {1}{U} instant — return target creature to its owner's hand; untap two lands. A tempo play that pays for itself — returning a threat and untapping mana to hold up a counterspell or cast another discard spell in the same turn.
*   **Dark Ritual:** {B} instant — add {B}{B}{B} to your mana pool. The most explosive turn-1 accelerant in the format. Leads into a turn-1 Bone Miser or a turn-2 Windfall before any opponent has set up.
*   **Withering Torment:** A discard-adjacent effect that drains life or depletes resources — contributes to the incremental pressure strategy by wearing down opponents outside of combat.

### ⚙️ Permanents & Equipment (2 Cards)
*   **Lightning Greaves:** {2} artifact — equipped creature gains shroud and haste. Equip {0}. Attaches for free — the moment Norman Osborn enters, Lightning Greaves protects him from targeted removal and gives him haste to attack for Neheb-style mana or to trigger any attack-based ability.
*   **Brass's Tunnel-Grinder / Tecutlan, the Searing Rift:** {3}{R} artifact. Front side processes the graveyard or generates value from discarding. Transforms into Tecutlan, the Searing Rift — a land that taps for mana and provides ongoing value. An artifact-to-land MDFC that provides ramp once the early game value engine is established.

### 🌀 Modal Double-Faced Spell (1 Card)
*   **Sink into Stupor / Soporific Springs:** {1}{U}{U} sorcery front / land back. Sink into Stupor bounces a creature and gives it "can't attack next turn." Soporific Springs is a land that taps for {U} and can be tapped to slow an attacking creature. Flexible at any point — cast the bounce mode early or play the land back when an extra mana source is needed.

### 🪨 Mana Rocks (4 Cards)
*   **Sol Ring:** One mana for two. The most important turn-1 play in the format.
*   **Arcane Signet:** Two mana, taps for any color in Norman Osborn's identity. The most versatile rock in a three-color deck.
*   **Talisman of Creativity:** {2} artifact — taps for {U} or {R} (1 life for off-color). Efficient early rock for Izzet-colored spells and wheel effects.
*   **Talisman of Indulgence:** {2} artifact — taps for {B} or {R} (1 life for off-color). Covers the Rakdos half of the color identity — mana for reanimation, removal, and discard payoffs.

## 🚀 Future Roadmap: Upgrades & Considerations

### 💡 High-Impact Tech to Consider
*   **Wheel of Fortune ({R}{R}):** The red Windfall — the most efficient wheel in the format at two mana. Adds a third wheel at the same power level as Windfall.
*   **Narset, Parter of Veils ({1}{U}{U}):** ⚠️ Game Changer (would bring total to 3). Prevents opponents from drawing extra cards while wheels give only you the extra draws. The ultimate wheel asymmetry piece.
*   **Notion Thief ({2}{U}{B}):** ⚠️ Game Changer (would bring total to 3). Flash creature — whenever opponents would draw cards, you draw instead. Cast in response to an opponent's draw spell to steal their entire hand refill. Devastating with opponent-targeted wheels.
*   **Teferi's Ageless Insight ({2}{U}{U}):** Whenever you draw a card, draw an additional card. With wheels drawing 5–7 cards, doubles the draw — turns Windfall into drawing 14 cards.
*   **Waste Not ({1}{B}):** Whenever an opponent discards a creature, create a 2/2 Zombie. Nonland = add {B}{B}. Land = draw a card. The symmetrical payoff for wheels — every card opponents discard in a Windfall becomes a Zombie, mana, or card for you.

### ⚠️ Bracket Notes
*   **Current Game Changers (2/3):** Cyclonic Rift, Fierce Guardianship.
*   Adding Narset **or** Notion Thief reaches the Bracket 3 maximum of 3 Game Changers. Adding both would push the deck to Bracket 4.
*   Mana Drain and Deflecting Swat are not on the current Game Changers list but significantly elevate the power ceiling — discuss with your pod.
