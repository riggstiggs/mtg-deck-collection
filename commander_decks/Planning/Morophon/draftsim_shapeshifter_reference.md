# Draftsim Reference: The 45 Best Shapeshifters in MTG Ranked

**Original Article:** [The 45 Best Shapeshifters in Magic Ranked - Draftsim](https://draftsim.com/mtg-shapeshifter/)

This document provides a detailed breakdown of the top 45 shapeshifters and changelings as ranked by Draftsim, along with key payoff cards and strategies.

---

## 🏆 The 45 Best Shapeshifters Ranked

### 45. [Crib Swap](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Crib+Swap)
*   **Role:** Removal / Kindred Spell
*   **Why it’s good:** An instant-speed exile removal spell that counts as a shapeshifter/kindred spell.
*   **How it’s used:** Used as spot removal in white decks.
*   **Key Synergies:** Best in decks that care about "kindred" spells or specific creature types, as it triggers effects related to shapeshifters.

### 44. [Irregular Cohort](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Irregular+Cohort)
*   **Role:** Token Generator
*   **Why it’s good:** Provides two 2/2 bodies with every creature type for 4 mana.
*   **How it’s used:** Primarily used in token-based strategies.
*   **Key Synergies:** Excellent for "blink" effects to generate more tokens; the bodies can serve as Soldiers, Angels, or any type needed for tribal bonuses.

### 43. [Amoeboid Changeling](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Amoeboid+Changeling)
*   **Role:** Utility / Type Manipulation
*   **Why it’s good:** A 2-drop that can manipulate creature types on the fly.
*   **How it’s used:** Used to grant or remove creature types from your own or your opponents' creatures.
*   **Key Synergies:** Can nullify an opponent's tribal bonuses by removing their creature types.

### 42. [Omni-Changeling](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Omni-Changeling)
*   **Role:** Flexible Clone
*   **Why it’s good:** A clone effect that features the **convoke** mechanic.
*   **How it’s used:** Can be cast very cheaply by tapping your existing creatures.
*   **Key Synergies:** Works well in go-wide decks where you have many creatures to tap for mana.

### 41. [Flock Impostor](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Flock+Impostor)
*   **Role:** Protection / Evasion
*   **Why it’s good:** A 3-mana creature with flash, flying, and a bounce ability.
*   **How it’s used:** Acts as a protection spell to save your valuable creatures from removal.
*   **Key Synergies:** Fits into any white-based tribal deck that needs evasive threats and protection.

### 40. [Stunt Double](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Stunt+Double)
*   **Role:** Flash Clone
*   **Why it’s good:** A strictly better Clone because it has **flash**.
*   **How it’s used:** Can be used as a surprise blocker or to copy a powerful creature an opponent just played.
*   **Key Synergies:** Functions as pseudo-removal by copying an attacker and blocking it.

### 39. [Callidus Assassin](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Callidus+Assassin)
*   **Role:** Removal / Clone
*   **Why it’s good:** Combines a clone effect with a removal spell.
*   **How it’s used:** Can be cast with flash to destroy a creature and then become a copy of it.
*   **Key Synergies:** In the worst case, it acts as a 3/3 flash "Ravenous Chupacabra."

### 38. [Copycrook](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Copycrook)
*   **Role:** Value / Scaling Clone
*   **Why it’s good:** A clone that utilizes the **connive** mechanic.
*   **How it’s used:** It loots (draw/discard) and grows with +1/+1 counters upon entering.
*   **Key Synergies:** Synergizes with Raffine, Scheming Seer and other discard/counter-based strategies.

### 37. [Evil Twin](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Evil+Twin)
*   **Role:** Political Removal
*   **Why it’s good:** A clone that can tap to destroy the creature it is copying.
*   **How it’s used:** Used for political leverage and repeatable removal of a specific threat.
*   **Key Synergies:** Excellent in Dimir control decks that want to punish opponents for playing high-value creatures.

### 36. [Bloodline Pretender](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Bloodline+Pretender)
*   **Role:** Scaling Threat
*   **Why it’s good:** A colorless creature that grows whenever another creature of a chosen type enters the battlefield.
*   **How it’s used:** A flexible inclusion for any tribal deck that floods the board.
*   **Key Synergies:** Humans, Soldiers, or any "go-wide" tribal strategy.

### 35. [Duplicant](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Duplicant)
*   **Role:** Colorless Removal
*   **Why it’s good:** Colorless removal that exiles a creature and adopts its power/toughness.
*   **How it’s used:** Can be slotted into any deck regardless of color.
*   **Key Synergies:** Artifact-heavy decks or decks needing non-color-restricted exile removal.

### 34. [Koh, the Face Stealer](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Koh,+the+Face+Stealer)
*   **Role:** Graveyard Hate / Utility
*   **Why it’s good:** Exiles creatures that die and allows you to shapeshift into them later.
*   **How it’s used:** Acts as graveyard hate while building a "reserve" of powerful forms.
*   **Key Synergies:** Synergizes with removal-heavy decks to ensure a steady supply of "faces" to steal.

### 33. [Flesh Duplicate](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Flesh+Duplicate)
*   **Role:** Efficient Clone
*   **Why it’s good:** Extremely cheap (2 mana) clone effect.
*   **How it’s used:** Used for temporary copies of powerful creatures or to trigger ETB effects.
*   **Key Synergies:** Proliferate (to keep it around longer) or creatures with powerful Enter-the-Battlefield triggers.

### 32. [Activated Sleeper](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Activated+Sleeper)
*   **Role:** Reactive Clone
*   **Why it’s good:** A cheap (3 mana) black clone that can copy a creature that went to the graveyard this turn.
*   **How it’s used:** Can copy creatures that were milled or destroyed.
*   **Key Synergies:** Self-mill strategies or decks that run heavy removal.

### 31. [Osgood, Operation Double](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Osgood,+Operation+Double)
*   **Role:** Ramp / Card Advantage
*   **Why it’s good:** Creates two creatures with mana abilities and has the **paradox** ability.
*   **How it’s used:** Provides mana ramp and card advantage via Clue tokens.
*   **Key Synergies:** Artifact decks and decks that cast spells from anywhere other than their hand.

### 30. [Firdoch Core](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Firdoch+Core)
*   **Role:** Ramp / Threat
*   **Why it’s good:** A mana rock that can transform into a large creature.
*   **How it’s used:** Provides mana fixing early and becomes a threat late-game.
*   **Key Synergies:** Tribal decks like Voja, Jaws of the Conclave that benefit from specific creature types.

### 29. [Masked Vandal](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Masked+Vandal)
*   **Role:** Utility / Removal
*   **Why it’s good:** A cheap green creature that exiles an artifact or enchantment on ETB.
*   **How it’s used:** A tribal-friendly way to handle problematic non-creature permanents.
*   **Key Synergies:** Elves, Giants, or Treefolk decks.

### 28. [Graveshifter](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Graveshifter)
*   **Role:** Recursion
*   **Why it’s good:** A "Gravedigger" with changeling.
*   **How it’s used:** Returns a creature from your graveyard to your hand.
*   **Key Synergies:** Zombie tribal or any deck needing creature recursion that also cares about creature types.

### 27. [Vesuvan Drifter](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Vesuvan+Drifter)
*   **Role:** Top-Deck Synergy
*   **Why it’s good:** A 2/4 flyer for 3 mana that can copy the top creature of your library.
*   **How it’s used:** Provides early evasion and late-game power by transforming into your deck's biggest threats.
*   **Key Synergies:** Top-deck manipulation (Scry, Surveil).

### 26. [Pirated Copy](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Pirated+Copy)
*   **Role:** Card Draw / Clone
*   **Why it’s good:** A clone that adds a "Curiosity" effect (drawing cards on combat damage).
*   **How it’s used:** Copies creatures with evasion to ensure card draw.
*   **Key Synergies:** Flying, Menace, or Trample creatures.

### 25. [Identity Thief](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Identity+Thief)
*   **Role:** Flicker / Clone
*   **Why it’s good:** Can copy a creature and flicker it simultaneously.
*   **How it’s used:** Used to reset counters, re-trigger ETBs, or temporarily remove a blocker.
*   **Key Synergies:** Creatures with powerful ETB effects or +1/+1 counter strategies.

### 24. [Unsettled Mariner](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Unsettled+Mariner)
*   **Role:** Protection / Tax
*   **Why it’s good:** A changeling "hate bear" that taxes opponents for targeting your permanents.
*   **How it’s used:** Protection for your board state in tribal or stax decks.
*   **Key Synergies:** Humans, Angels, or UW control/stax.

### 23. [Nascent Metamorph](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Nascent+Metamorph)
*   **Role:** Chaos / Copy
*   **Why it’s good:** A high-risk, high-reward 2-drop that copies the top card of an opponent's library when it attacks or blocks.
*   **How it’s used:** Used to deter attacks or gamble on becoming a massive threat.
*   **Key Synergies:** Opponents playing creature-heavy decks.

### 22. [Valiant Changeling](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Valiant+Changeling)

## 💎 Shapeshifter Payoffs & Strategies

*   **Typal Synergy Boosters:** Changelings are "jacks-of-all-trades" that fit into any tribal deck. **[Springleaf Parade](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Springleaf+Parade)** is specifically mentioned as a way to boost your ranks of typal creatures.
*   **Mana Curve Smoothing:** Low-cost changelings like **Changeling Outcast** and **[Masked Vandal](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Masked+Vandal)** help enable tribal synergies early for expensive tribes like Dragons, Dinosaurs, or Giants.
*   **Legendary Cloning Strategies:**
    *   **[Keiga, the Tide Star](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Keiga,+the+Tide+Star):** Cloning Keiga triggers the legend rule; you keep one, the other dies, and you get a "Mind Control" death trigger.
    *   **[Hidetsugu and Kairi](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Hidetsugu+and+Kairi):** Copying this provides both ETB and death triggers for massive card draw and value.
*   **Gyruda Combo:** Using **[Gyruda, Doom of Depths](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Gyruda,+Doom+of+Depths)** to mill and hit clones (like **[Spark Double](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Spark+Double)** which ignores the legend rule) allows you to chain ETB triggers until you hit a finisher like **[Craterhoof Behemoth](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Craterhoof+Behemoth)**.
*   **Powerful Noncreature Spells:**
    *   **[Harmonized Crescendo](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Harmonized+Crescendo)**, **[The Bears of Littjara](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!The+Bears+of+Littjara)**, and **[Peer Pressure](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Peer+Pressure)** benefit significantly from having many shapeshifters.
    *   **One-Sided Board Wipes:** **[Kindred Dominance](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Kindred+Dominance)** and **[Raise the Palisade](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Raise+the+Palisade)** become devastating when your board consists of shapeshifters.
*   **Mechanic Enablers:**
    *   **Outlaws:** Changelings count as Assassins, Mercenaries, Pirates, Rogues, and Warlocks.
    *   **Party:** A single changeling can fill any one of the required roles (Wizard, Warrior, Rogue, or Cleric) to help achieve a "full party" bonus. **[Stick Together](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Stick+Together)** and **[Double Down](https://scryfall.com/search?q=!Double+Down)** are key enablers.
