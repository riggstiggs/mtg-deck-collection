
# Brackets 

----

### Bracket 1 - Exhibition

- Casual Mindset
- Thematic decks over powerful decks

#### The Difference

*How’s this different from Bracket 2 – Core?*
“Moving to Core introduces mechanical staples and more reliable interaction, shifting away from pure flavor toward functional gameplay.”

#### Philosophy & Intent
Decks built to show off something unusual — a specific story, creature type, or wacky mechanic. Winning is secondary to the experience.

#### Game Pace
Expected Game Length: **10+ Turns**
Every player at the table must complete at least **9** full turns before any player can win. The earliest possible win could occur on the 10th turn. This ensures slow, thematic gameplay where winning is secondary to the experience. Note: this does not account for rare perfect opening hands and/or draws that may allow a deck to explode past its usual potential.

#### Defining the Restrictions

- **NO Game Changers** - Game Changers are a curated list of high-impact cards maintained by the Commander Format Panel that dramatically warp casual games. Brackets 1 and 2 prohibit all Game Changers.

- **NO Mass Land Denial** - Cards or strategies that regularly destroy, exile, bounce, keep tapped, or change what mana is produced by four or more lands per player without replacing them. This includes classic MLD like Armageddon and Ruination, as well as stax effects like Winter Orb and Blood Moon. Single-target land removal (e.g., Strip Mine) is not MLD unless the effect can be looped.

- **No Extra Turns** - Extra-turn spells and abilities are not allowed at this bracket. Even a single extra turn can be too disruptive for purely thematic play.

- **No 2-Card Combos** - An intentional combination of exactly two cards that creates an infinite loop, locks out the game, or instantly wins. Examples include Thassa’s Oracle + Demonic Consultation. Brackets 1 and 2 prohibit these entirely.

#### Power Feel
Lands are basics-heavy or thematically chosen over efficient duals. Ramp is slow or creature-based. Card draw is sparse and often tied to the deck’s theme. Synergies exist but are incidental — the deck is built around a story or creature type, not around winning. A Bracket 1 deck should feel like it could have been built by someone who only owns cards related to their theme.

### Bracket 2 - Core

- Casual Mindset
- Creativity & Entertainment

#### The Difference

*How’s this different from Bracket 1 – Exhibition?*
“Core decks differ from Exhibition by prioritizing mechanical cohesion and reliable card choices over purely thematic elements.”

*How’s this different from Bracket 3 – Upgraded?*
“Upgraded decks introduce access to Game Changers (up to 3) and allow late-game 2-card combos, shifting toward proactive threats and efficient finishers.”

#### Philosophy & Intent
The baseline casual Commander experience. Decks are mechanically coherent and interactive without ending the game abruptly.

#### Game Pace
Expected Game Length: **9+ Turns**
Every player at the table must complete at least 8 full turns before any player can win. The earliest possible win could occur on the 9th turn. Decks are mechanically coherent but not built to close games quickly. Note: this does not account for rare perfect opening hands and/or draws that may allow a deck to explode past its usual potential.

#### Defining the Restrictions

- **NO Game Changers** - Game Changers are a curated list of high-impact cards maintained by the Commander Format Panel that dramatically warp casual games. Brackets 1 and 2 prohibit all Game Changers.

- **NO Mass Land Denial** - Cards or strategies that regularly destroy, exile, bounce, keep tapped, or change what mana is produced by four or more lands per player without replacing them. This includes classic MLD like Armageddon and Ruination, as well as stax effects like Winter Orb and Blood Moon. Single-target land removal (e.g., Strip Mine) is not MLD unless the effect can be looped.

- **No Chaining Extra Turns** - Extra-turn cards are allowed in low quantities, but they must not be chained in succession or looped. A single extra turn is fine; building a deck to recur or copy extra turn spells for multiple consecutive turns is not.

- **No 2-Card Combos** - An intentional combination of exactly two cards that creates an infinite loop, locks out the game, or instantly wins. Examples include Thassa’s Oracle + Demonic Consultation. Brackets 1 and 2 prohibit these entirely.

#### Power Feel
This is where most "kitchen table" Commander decks live. Lands work but aren’t optimized (a few duals, mostly basics or tap-lands). Ramp is present but leans on Signets, Talismans, and basic land fetchers — not explosive rocks. Card draw exists but isn’t relentless. Synergies are real and functional but not maximally tight. **Passing the Bracket 2 restriction checklist is not enough to classify a deck as Bracket 2.** A deck with a fully optimized mana base, 10 efficient ramp pieces, 12 draw engines, and tightly tuned synergies is a Bracket 3 deck even if it contains zero Game Changers — it simply plays above the bracket’s expected power level at the table.

### Bracket 3 - Upgraded

- Casual Mindset
- Synergy & Quality

#### The Difference

*How’s this different from Bracket 2 – Core?*
“The leap from Core to Upgraded introduces access to Game Changers and late-game 2-card combos. Decks become less ‘fair’ and more explosive.”

*How’s this different from Bracket 4 – Optimized?*
“Optimized decks remove all bracket restrictions. Mass Land Denial, early combos, and unlimited Game Changers are all on the table.”

#### Philosophy & Intent
Souped-up decks with careful card selection and efficient staples. Players expect strong plays, meaningful interaction, and access to a limited number of Game Changers.

#### Game Pace
Expected Game Length: **7+ Turns**
Every player at the table must complete at least 6 full turns before any player can win. The earliest possible win could occur on the 7th turn. Decks can be efficient, but combos and decisive plays should not resolve before this threshold. Note: this does not account for rare perfect opening hands and/or draws that may allow a deck to explode past its usual potential.

#### Defining the Restrictions

- **0-3 Game Changers** - Bracket 3 allows a maximum of three cards from the Game Changers list. This provides access to powerful staples like Demonic Tutor or Cyclonic Rift while keeping the overall density of game-warping effects in check.

- **NO Mass Land Denial** - Cards or strategies that regularly destroy, exile, bounce, keep tapped, or change what mana is produced by four or more lands per player without replacing them. This includes classic MLD like Armageddon and Ruination, as well as stax effects like Winter Orb and Blood Moon. Single-target land removal (e.g., Strip Mine) is not MLD unless the effect can be looped.

- **No Chaining Extra Turns** - Extra-turn cards are allowed in low quantities, but they must not be chained in succession or looped. A single extra turn is fine; building a deck to recur or copy extra turn spells for multiple consecutive turns is not.

- **No 2-Card Combos** - An intentional combination of exactly two cards that creates an infinite loop, locks out the game, or instantly wins. Examples include Thassa’s Oracle + Demonic Consultation. Brackets 1 and 2 prohibit these entirely.

#### Power Feel
Decks are consciously optimized: efficient mana base (shocks, fetches, or budget equivalents), consistent ramp, reliable card advantage, and synergies that actually form an engine. Players have made deliberate choices to maximize each card slot. This is where most "I tuned my deck" Commander decks actually land, even when the builder was aiming for Bracket 2. The presence of 0–3 Game Changers is a signal, but so is overall construction quality — a deck built with the intent to reliably execute its gameplan by turn 6–7 belongs here regardless of its Game Changer count.

### Bracket 4 - Optimized

- Competetive Mindset
- Lethal & Consistent

#### The Difference

*How’s this different from Bracket 3 – Upgraded?*
“Optimized decks remove all bracket restrictions. Mass Land Denial, unlimited Game Changers, and early 2-card combos are all allowed.”

*How’s this different from Bracket 5 – cEDH?*
“cEDH adds a competitive, metagame-focused mindset. Decks are tuned to interact with specific tier-0 strategies using zero-cost interaction and hyper-efficient loops.”

#### Philosophy & Intent
The highest-power Commander decks most players will encounter. No bracket restrictions apply — any legal card and strategy is fair game.

#### Game Pace
Expected Game Length: **5+ Turns** 
Every player at the table must complete at least 4 full turns before any player can win. The earliest possible win could occur on the 5th turn. Beyond this threshold, any legal strategy is fair game. Note: this does not account for rare perfect opening hands and/or draws that may allow a deck to explode past its usual potential.

#### Defining the Restrictions

- **No Deck Restrictions** - No bracket-specific restrictions on card selection. Any card legal in the Commander format is allowed, and efficiency is the primary deckbuilding metric.

#### Power Feel
Decks are built against a known competitive metagame. Card choices are informed by tier lists, known combo lines, and interaction density. Zero-cost interaction (Force of Will, Fierce Guardianship), hyper-efficient loops, and the ability to win or stop a win on any turn are baseline expectations.

#### Power Feel
Every card is pulling maximum weight. Mana base is near-perfect, ramp is fast (Mana Vault, Ancient Tomb tier), tutors are plentiful, and the deck is built to close games quickly and consistently. No card earns its slot through flavor or fun — everything is there to win.

### Bracket 5 - cEDH

- Competetive Mindset
- Meticulous & Strategic

#### The Difference

*How’s this different from Bracket 4 – Optimized?*
“While Optimized decks are powerful, cEDH decks are built specifically to interact with the metagame — tuned for tier-0 combos, zero-cost interaction, and hyper-efficient resource loops.”

#### Philosophy & Intent
The ceiling of the format. Combines the highest power level with a competitive, metagame-focused mindset. Games could end on any turn.

#### Game Pace
Expected Game Length: **None** 
There is no turn restriction. A player can win on any turn, including turn 1. Games are expected to be fast, and every deck should be prepared to interact from the very first turn cycle.

#### Defining the Restrictions

- **No Deck Restrictions** - No bracket-specific restrictions on card selection. Any card legal in the Commander format is allowed, and efficiency is the primary deckbuilding metric.
