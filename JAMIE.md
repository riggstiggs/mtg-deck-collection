## The one-sentence identity

> **Jamie builds flavorful, synergy-first value engines that snowball through many
> small interactions until the board quietly belongs to her.** She starts with the
> *idea*, then connects pieces until every card belongs — the same way she builds
> projects at work.

---

## Core playstyle

- **Green is home.** Almost always in the deck. Simic (GU) is her current wheelhouse
  (Adrix & Nev Twincaster is her favorite deck; both Zimone decks are beloved).
  Ranges across the green guilds: Selesnya (GW), Golgari (BG), and a soft spot for
  Gruul (RG) from earlier days. **Can** play red and black, but green is the anchor.
- **Value engine midrange.** Build a machine that gets a little more ridiculous every
  turn. Incremental advantage, not explosive combo. "My board gets better each turn,"
  not "here's my 14/14, deal with it."
- **Go WIDE, not tall.** Tokens, a busy battlefield, lots of permanents. Loves
  doubling/multiplying effects (Doubling Season, Parallel Lives, Second Harvest,
  Adrix & Nev, Esix, Chatterfang). "Make more of the thing" is a signature.
- **Loves:** lots of mana (hence green), going wide, a good **trample** payoff, and
  **drawing cards**. Engines that chain. Commanders that are a *puzzle to build
  around*, not just "play good cards in these colors."
- **Aristocrats / Food / ETB chains / graveyard recursion** all land well **when they
  tell a story** — flavor-first, not power-first.

## Flavor is a first-class requirement

- **Theme matters as much as mechanics.** She'd rather run the perfect-fit card than a
  generic staple that's 5% stronger. Every card should feel like it belongs to the idea.
- **Math is a genuine identity marker.** Former double-math-major and alg/geo teacher.
  Nods to math (Zimone, fractals, Mathemagics) genuinely delight her — lean into them
  when a theme allows.
- The deck should *do a thing* and *tell a story*. Start from "what's the idea?" not
  "what's the strongest card?"

## Hard constraints (these are load-bearing — respect them)

1. **She is the HOST.** During games she's up getting snacks, letting the dogs out,
   keeping the table going. **The deck must run well while she's not glued to it.**
   Fun triggers are welcome, but avoid decks that require constant hold-up mana,
   dense every-turn reactive decisions, or heavy stack interaction. 1–3 triggers a
   turn is the sweet spot; a Fantastic-Four level of triggers is the *upper* comfort
   bound, not the target.
2. **"Quiet until it isn't."** She likes to look non-threatening and be left alone to
   build her engine, then take over. Decks that telegraph a threat get her commander
   targeted — which she dislikes both tactically and because of #3.
3. **Recastable commander / low commander tax exposure.** She wants to be able to
   physically afford to recast her commander. Don't build around a commander so
   expensive or so targeted that repeated recasts sink the game. Protect the
   commander or keep its cost recastable.
4. **Budget-conscious.** Karametra taught the lesson: *"I refuse to pay that much for
   a deck moving forward."* Prefer budget-to-mid builds. Always be able to show the
   cheaper substitution and the deck's total cost (use `manapool_price_deck.py`).
   Flag when a card is a big price spike and offer a budget alt.
5. **Consistency over max intricacy.** She likes an engine, not homework. When a deck
   "isn't consistent" (past pain: Hazel, Morophon), tightening reliability is the job
   — WITHOUT turning it into a 40-moving-part Mark machine.

## Hard NOs

- Stax, lockdown, land destruction, prison — anything that wins by making everyone
  else miserable. She does not want to stop people from playing; she wants to build.
- Heavy counterspell / hard-control shells (a single flexible counter is fine; a
  counter *suite* is not — also conflicts with being the host, #1).
- Pure/linear aggro, mono-red burn-face, Voltron.
- Fast combo, storm, "oops I win" / infinite-combo kills, cEDH optimization.
  Winning because the engine finally came together is the point; winning by combo
  is hollow to her.

## Build style & workflow

- **Precon → upgrade** is her default and preferred path. Every owned deck started as
  a precon she made better. Respect the precon's identity while tightening it; don't
  rebuild from scratch unless she asks.
- Mark (her husband) usually builds her decks; the point of this project is Jamie
  building her own. Don't drift her ideas toward "a Mark deck" (40 moving parts, max
  complexity, from-scratch). Keep it a *Jamie* deck.
